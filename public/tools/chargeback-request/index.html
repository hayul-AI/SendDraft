Understanding Chargebacks

A chargeback is a consumer protection mechanism that allows you to dispute a transaction and have the funds returned by your card issuer. It is intended for cases where a merchant fails to deliver a product or service, or when a charge is fraudulent. A formal, written request to your bank is often the first step in the official dispute process.

FAQ

What are valid reasons for a chargeback?

Common valid reasons include fraudulent transactions, products that were never delivered, services that didn't match their description, and technical errors like duplicate charges.

How long does the chargeback process take?

The entire process can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months, depending on the complexity of the dispute and the responsiveness of the merchant.
