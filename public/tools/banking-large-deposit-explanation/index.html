Explaining Large Deposits

When applying for a mortgage or a large loan, underwriters carefully review your bank statements. They often require a written explanation for any "large" or "atypical" deposits to ensure the funds are legitimate and do not represent undisclosed debt. A clear, factual explanation helps move your application forward without unnecessary delays.

FAQ

What counts as a "large" deposit?

In most mortgage reviews, a large deposit is defined as any single deposit that exceeds 25% to 50% of your total monthly qualifying income. However, requirements vary by lender and loan type.

Do I always need a gift letter?

If the source of funds is a gift from a family member, lenders almost always require a signed "Gift Letter" in addition to your explanation. Use this tool to provide the initial clarification.
