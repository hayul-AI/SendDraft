Credit Dispute Basics

Under federal law (like the FCRA in the US), you have the right to dispute any information on your credit report that you believe is inaccurate. The reporting agency is required to investigate your claim and correct or remove any information that cannot be verified. A formal, written dispute is the most effective way to start this process.

FAQ

How long does a credit dispute take?

In most cases, credit bureaus have 30 days to investigate and respond to your dispute. If they find the information is indeed inaccurate, they must update your report immediately.

What documents should I provide?

Supporting documents like monthly statements, payment receipts, or a copy of your ID can significantly speed up the investigation process by providing immediate verification of your claim.
